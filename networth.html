NET WORTH TRACKER

Redesigning a personal finance app for clarity and trust

product design UI UX design system

Product design · UI/UX · Design system, 2026

The Brief A personal net worth tracker for the financially literate, FIRE-minded investor — someone who wants to check their finances at a glance and, now and then, dive deep. The existing app worked, but it read like a spreadsheet: no visual hierarchy, inconsistent number formatting, and the features people used most were buried next to the ones they rarely touched. The goal was to rebuild trust through clarity. [Replace with your own framing of the problem and constraints.]

The Approach I led with a single principle: an element's prominence should match how often it's used and how relevant it is in that moment — checking-mode first, deep-dive second. Colour was tied to meaning rather than decoration: the interface stays greyscale except where money moves, with lime for gains and orange for losses. A token-first design system produced light and dark modes from one source of truth. [Add your process, key decisions, and the trade-offs you made.]
